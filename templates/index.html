Judging A Book By Its Cover


The goal of this analysis is to gain the ability to predict the genre of a book by viewing its cover.

For our final project, we have trained a Convolutional Neural Network with the goal of predicting a book's genre based solely on an image of the book's cover. We utilized new technologies learned in class and through external research. Data was assembled and manipulated using Python and Pandas, the model was trained using Keras and a TensorFlow backend, and visualizations of the model's performance are displayed in this Flask app using JavaScript (D3 and Plotly), HTML and CSS.

Below is a list of all Categories:

Arts & Photography, Biographies & Memoirs, Business & Money, Calendars, Children's Books, Comics & Graphic Novels, Computers & Technology, Cookbooks, Food & Wine, Crafts, Hobbies & Home, Christian Books & Bibles, Engineering & Transportation, Health, Fitness & Dieting, History, Humor & Entertainment, Law, Literature & Fiction, Medical Books, Mystery, Thriller & Suspense, Parenting & Relationships, Politics & Social Sciences, Reference, Religion & Spirituality, Romance, Science & Math, Science Fiction & Fantasy, Self-Help, Sports & Outdoors, Teen & Young Adult, Test Preparation, and Travel.

Our image data comes from a dataset created by researchers at Kyushu University in Japan. The full dataset contains more than 200,000 images across the 30 categories. You can see the distribution among these categories in the below chart. For the purposes of training, validation and testing, samples were taken that were balanced across all 30 categories.
